Frequently Asked Questions

What are the admission requirements for the ECE program?

To be eligible for admission to the ECE program, candidates must have completed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics with a minimum aggregate of 60%. Additionally, candidates must qualify in the university entrance examination.

What is the duration of the ECE program?

The Bachelor's program in Electronics and Computer Engineering is a 4-year program divided into 8 semesters. Each semester typically lasts for 6 months.

What are the major specializations available in ECE?

Our department offers specializations in VLSI Design, Embedded Systems, Communication Systems, Computer Networks, and Artificial Intelligence. Students can choose their specialization in the final year of the program.

What kind of projects do students work on?

Students work on various projects including IoT applications, Robotics, Signal Processing, Machine Learning applications, and Hardware Design. We encourage interdisciplinary projects that combine electronics and computer engineering concepts.

What laboratory facilities are available?

Our department has state-of-the-art laboratories including Digital Electronics Lab, Microprocessor Lab, VLSI Lab, Communication Lab, and Computer Networks Lab. All labs are equipped with modern equipment and software tools.

What are the career prospects after completing ECE?

Graduates can pursue careers in various fields including VLSI Design, Embedded Systems, Telecommunications, Software Development, IoT, and Research & Development. Our department has strong industry connections and provides placement assistance.

Does the department provide internship opportunities?

Yes, we have tie-ups with various industries and research organizations for summer internships. Students are encouraged to undertake internships during their summer breaks to gain practical experience.
